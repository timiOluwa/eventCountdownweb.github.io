EVENT TRIBES

An event tribe refers to a group of people who share a common interest or passion and regularly come together to participate in related events. These tribes are characterized by their strong sense of community and shared identity, often centered around specific activities, interests, or causes. Members of an event tribe are typically deeply engaged and invested in their shared interest, fostering a sense of belonging and connection through their participation in events.

Music festival enthusiasts form distinct event tribes, each contributing to the rich tapestry of festival culture. These tribes are characterized by shared identities, fashion, and values, creating a sense of community among attendees. Die-hard fans are deeply dedicated to specific artists or genres, following their favorite musicians on tour and wearing fan gear to connect with the music and artists they adore. Ravers, synonymous with EDM festivals, are known for their high-energy, neon-colored outfits and communal activities like kandi trading, embodying the values of Peace, Love, Unity, and Respect (PLUR). Modern festival hippies, rooted in 1960s counterculture, embrace peace, love, and freedom, often attending events like Burning Man, where they emphasize communal living, self-expression, and sustainability.
